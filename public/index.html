Professional Projects

The NOAA Great Lakes Level Viewer is a tool that's part of the inundation series of app's hosted by The Office for Coastal Management. I led the development team on this project while mentoring a junior developer. We interfaced with the Physical Scientists involved in the management of the data to ensure maximum integrity and accuracy of the simulations. The app was showcased to the US Congress and other government bodies with great reception.

  • Built a tool under federal directive for simulating shore inundations of the US Great Lakes.
  • Made publicly available for decision-making by city planners, residents, and civil engineers.
  • Utilized link sharing and dynamic application usage through base-64 strings appended to the URL allow users to share the exact location and water-level.
  • Utilized OpenLayers, Highcharts, and a pixel-rendering system to display various water levels on a variety of charts and map types.
  • Provided a Social Vulnerability layer for users via CDC API, and a Depth Query feature to query multiple points of water levles in the inundation zone or the shoreline.

The NOAA Economic Ocean Watch Explorer (ENOW) displays data on all the coastal states and counties of the Continental United States, as well as six US Territories. The same data is also served to a tool called Ocean Reports that allows users to filter and download the data in csv formats. Spanning sectors from Tourism and Recreation, to Offshore Mining, to Ship Building and Construction, ENOW allows users to gather comprehensive data on their state, county, or territory

  • Explore over 15 years of economic data for coastal communities, including 6 U.S. Territories.
  • Featuring a seamless user experience with a responsive single-page application that dynamically parses and displays data using HighCharts.
  • Spans six economic sectors and four data categories across 30 coastal states, 400+ marine counties over 16 years, and in a recent update, covers data on six US Territories from the last three years.
  • The tool offers more than 165,000 unique data combinations, enabling tailored data exploration to suit diverse needs.
  • Each combination of data is shown on three interactive charts and a statistics panel, offering over 660,000 distinct views to support social and economic professionals in understanding community dynamics.

At Kloudlog, I spearheaded the development of a full-stack React application that enables visual creation and editing of GCP resources such as Storage Buckets, Compute Engines, and Virtual Machines using a custom drag-and-drop interface. The user is able to create, test, and run various Terraform configurations of infrastructure modules.

  • Seamlessly integrates Terraform infrastructure, allowing users to build and manage GCP resources within a visually intuitive environment.

  • Real-time data updates are delivered via websocket, ensuring users always have the latest information at their fingertips.
  • Developed in a Microservices architecture to segregate application layers for optimal performance and scalability.
